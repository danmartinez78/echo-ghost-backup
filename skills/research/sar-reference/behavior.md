# Lost Pet Behavior Reference

**Purpose:** Understand what a lost pet is likely doing based on time elapsed, conditions, and species — so search strategy matches the behavioral reality.

---

## Dogs vs. Cats: Fundamental Differences

| Behavior | Dogs | Cats |
|----------|------|------|
| Response to humans | Seek attention, approach | Hide, freeze, flee |
| Movement style | Active, exploratory | Opportunistic, minimal |
| Scared response | Run and circle | Freeze in place |
| Food motivation | High | Moderate |
| Return instinct | Strong (home bond) | Moderate (territory) |
| Socialization benefit | Approachable if socialized | More elusive if feral |
| Vocalization | May bark, whine | Rarely vocalize when scared |

**Critical implication:** A scared cat will stay in a hiding spot even if you walk within 5 feet. Calling/shouting can make them freeze harder. Silent search (トラAPPING, motion cameras) is more effective for cats.

## Behavioral Phases

### Phase 1: PANIC / FLIGHT (0–30 minutes)

**What the pet is experiencing:** High cortisol, adrenaline spike. Not thinking — just moving. Instinct drives them away from the scary stimulus (door closing, loud noise, open gate).

**Typical behaviors:**
- Runs in a straight line along fences, roads, or open paths
- May jump over low obstacles they'd normally avoid
- Doesn't respond to name or commands
- Vocalizing (barking, whining) is common
- Will not stop voluntarily

**What this means for search:**
- Initial heading is key — interview owner/witnesses about direction of flight
- Search linear corridors FIRST: fence lines, roads, wood lines, creek beds
- Don't waste time searching yards and houses — the dog is still running

---

### Phase 2: EXHAUSTION / FREEZE (30 min – 6 hours)

**What the pet is experiencing:** Adrenaline crash. First wave of exhaustion. Finds a hiding spot or cover and stops moving.

**Typical behaviors:**
- Finds dark, enclosed space: under deck, dense bush, drainage pipe, hollow log
- Lies still — may be panting heavily
- Responds to owner's voice with subtle signs (ear prick, head turn) but rarely comes out
- Progressive behavioral shutdown

**What this means for search:**
- This is when grid searches become effective
- Focus on ANY enclosed/sheltered space within 1/2 mile of LKP
- Bring the pet's favorite toy or a worn item of owner's clothing — familiar scent can trigger recall
- Don't call loudly — a calm, normal voice is less threatening

---

### Phase 3: HUNGER / THIRST EMERGENCE (6–48 hours)

**What the pet is experiencing:** Biological needs start overriding fear. Pet becomes hungry, thirsty. May start moving at dawn/dusk when it's cooler.

**Typical behaviors:**
- Creeping movement during early morning (5–8am) and evening (6–9pm)
- Less fearful of humans if hungry enough
- May investigate yards, porches, garages if they smell food
- Dogs: may approach people asking for food
- Cats: will still hide but may emerge for food at night

**What this means for search:**
- Set feeding stations at LKP and along probable corridors (not inside a trap — that frightens them)
- Motion-activated cameras at feeding stations confirm presence
- Door-to-door canvas of neighbors most effective at this phase
- Early morning and dusk searches highest yield

---

### Phase 4: STRESS / LEARNED HELPLESSNESS (48+ hours)

**What the pet is experiencing:** Chronic stress. Behavior becomes less predictable. May alternate between freezing and brief active periods.

**Typical behaviors:**
- Reduced responsiveness to humans (both dogs and cats)
- More nocturnal movement pattern
- Dogs: may follow other animals or food sources
- Cats: may have found a territory with natural food (rats, birds, garbage)
- Some pets become more approachable; others go fully feral

**What this means for search:**
- Professional trailing/scent dog most effective
- humane traps with food/tomit bait become necessary for cats
- Broader radius, check locations with consistent food sources
- Social media flyering and community alerts most effective

## Temperature Effects

| Condition | Effect on Behavior |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Temp <40°F | Small dogs stop moving almost entirely. Cats become motionless. Search intensively near LKP. |
| Temp 40–55°F | Reduced but active. Dawn/dusk movement increases. |
| Temp 55–75°F | Normal activity pattern. All phases operate as described. |
| Temp 75–85°F | Movement shifts heavily to dawn/dusk. Rest during midday heat. |
| Temp >85°F | Pets seek water and shade. Look near sprinklers, pools, creek edges. Reduce daytime search. |
| Rain | Immediate shelter-seeking. Pet will hunker down and not move. |

## Dogs: Breed-Specific Notes

| Type | Behavioral Tendency |
|------|-------------------|
| Hounds (scent/trail) | Follow scent trails strongly. May circle back toward home on familiar scents. Good candidates for scent-trailing search. |
| Sight hounds (Greyhound, Borzoi) | Run fast in straight lines. Initial heading critical. May travel 2–3× normal distance in first hour. |
| Terriers (rat, JRT, etc.) | High prey drive. May chase animals. Can get into small spaces and voids. |
| Herding breeds | Strong homing instinct. May circle and try to return. Check home direction aggressively. |
| Brachycephalic (Bulldogs, Pugs) | Overheat quickly. Movement limited in warm weather. Check shaded areas first. |
| Giant breeds | More persistent movement. Can travel far. Less likely to seek shelter quickly. |
| Small/toy breeds | Freeze when scared. Often found within 1/4 mile despite owner's estimate of longer distance. |

## Cats: Indoor vs. Outdoor Access

### Indoor-Only Cats (highest risk, most disoriented)

- Terrified of outdoor environment
- Will squeeze into smallest available space immediately
- Most commonly found within 1/2 block of LKP — often under 100 feet
- Will NOT meow or respond to calling
- May be trapped by accident in garages, sheds, basements of neighbors
- **DO NOT** search wide radius first — exhaustive search of immediate area (every bush, under every porch) FIRST

### Cats with Outdoor Access

- More mobile and terrain-savvy
- May travel 2–5× farther than indoor-only
- Often found near other people's feeding stations, bird feeders, gardens
- More likely to approach a calm stranger for food

### Feral Cats

- Will not approach humans under any circumstance
- Travel greatest distances
- Most likely to have found a food source territory
- Humane trap with food bait is only effective capture method
- May require days of trap-setting before capture

## Signs of Pet Presence (What to Look For)

**Dogs:**
- Paw prints in mud/dirt
- Disturbed vegetation at fence lines
- Dog waste (fresh = recent presence)
- Barking/whining sounds (dawn/dusk)
- Community reports ("I saw a dog matching that description")

**Cats:**
- Scat (outdoor cats leave scat near territory boundaries)
- Paw prints in dusty areas
- Fur caught on fences, branches
- Cat-shaped resting spots in dust/soil
- Neighbor sightings (cats are seen more than owners realize)

---

## Owner Behavior and Emotional State

**Important intel context:** The owner is under extreme stress. Their timeline estimates are often wrong (they overestimate time since escape due to panic). Their search radius estimate tends to be too wide for cats, too narrow for dogs.

**Always:** Ask when they last saw the pet with certainty (doorbell cam footage, neighbor confirmation), not just their best guess.
